The stance

Most diet apps are slot
machines. This one is a gauge.

The engagement-bait playbook — guilt-red foods, streaks you're scared to break, a buzz every few hours — makes beginners under-log and quit. Cut Protocol bans all three, in writing, in the code.

Instead of the streak

Your data beats the model

The scale is the truth. When your real weight trend disagrees with the prediction, the app re-derives your target from what actually happened — not from a guilt trip.

Instead of the buzz

Every change is logged & reversible

Automatic adjustments are shown, never silent. You can see exactly why today's number moved, and undo it. No nudges, no notifications, no dark patterns.

Instead of the lock-in

Your data is never trapped

JSON and CSV export always work. It's a tool you own on your own machine — not an account someone can hold your history hostage inside.

The colour constitution

Three laws it can't break.

Every screen — and this page — obeys the same three rules about colour. They aren't a style; they're a stance about how a body-composition tool should make you feel.

a

Green is earned

The one green may only mean on-target: a hit goal, the primary action, the trend heading the right way. It's never spent on a selected tab or a category dot. Its power is that everything around it stays quiet.

On-target Selected = a step brighter, not green
c

Three macros, three colours, forever

Protein, carbs and fat get one fixed, colourblind-safe hue each — the Okabe-Ito triad — used identically in every ring, bar, chip and chart, always tagged with a letter. Those hues are never borrowed for anything else.

Red on food reads as moral judgment — so the ring simply laps past 100%, Apple-style. Go over, and the number turns a calm amber and the app starts re-planning. It never once turns red.

The solver

A week that hits the number —
or tells you why it can't.

From your validated food library it scores complete-day candidates on an honest match — 55% calories, 30% protein shortfall, the rest fat & carb range — respecting diet, allergies and prep time as hard filters, cuisine and budget as gentle biases.

Cut Protocol · Meal plan
▸The real Plan screen. Watch Saturday's salmon slot — the solver tried 5 recipes, none fit within tolerance, so it says so: "landed 731 kcal vs a 624 target," and points you at the swap button. Never a silent miss.

◆ Honest match %

Every day shows how close it landed. A soft day is flagged amber — never hidden behind a green checkmark it didn't earn.

↺ Three-alternate swap

Don't like a slot? Three compliance-checked alternates, and the server rebuilds the day's totals around your pick.

▲ Declares unsolvable — with reasons

If the pool can't hit target it says so and explains why. It never suggests loosening your allergies, and silent misses are forbidden.

No guessing

Every gram has a source.

Numbers are only as good as where they came from — so each food carries its provenance, and the sources are never silently mixed.
