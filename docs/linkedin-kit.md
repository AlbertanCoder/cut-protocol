## 3. The post ("what I've been working on" — not a launch)

I work construction, and on my nights and days off I've been building
something.

A while back I noticed that when people around me tried to diet, they didn't
quit because of willpower. They quit because of the homework: figuring out
their calories, splitting macros, planning meals that fit the numbers, every
single day. Nobody teaches that, and the apps that do it mostly hand you a
number and wish you luck.

So I've been building my own take on it. It's called Cut Protocol. You put in
your stats, your job, and how you train — it calculates your calories using
several published formulas (and shows you the math instead of hiding it),
then generates a weekly meal plan and grocery list that actually fit your
targets. If it can't hit your numbers, it tells you why instead of quietly
missing.

Honest caveats: it's a side project, it's early, and it'll be a work in
progress for a while. I'm not launching anything or selling anything — I just
think it's genuinely amazing what one person can build these days with AI
dev tools, and this is me finding out on a problem I care about.

Repo (code and screenshots): https://github.com/AlbertanCoder/cut-protocol
